The new command

After install:

dailystonks-refresh-universe --source sp500

That refreshes:

data/sp500_constituents.csv

The S&P 500 source includes Symbol, Security, GICS Sector, GICS Sub-Industry, Date added, and related columns.

Import your original big CSV

Use this when you want the standalone app to match the original DailyStonks universe exactly:

dailystonks-refresh-universe --source custom --input "D:\path\to\your\big_universe.csv"

It accepts common column names like:

Symbol
Ticker
Name
Company
Security
Sector
GICS Sector
Industry
GICS Sub-Industry
Exchange

It also makes a timestamped backup before overwriting the active universe CSV.

Broader US-listed universe
dailystonks-refresh-universe --source us-listed

With ETFs included:

dailystonks-refresh-universe --source us-listed --include-etfs

Trade-off: the broad NasdaqTrader universe has lots of symbols, but sector/industry will mostly be Unknown unless you import or enrich your own data. For sector-heavy DailyStonks cards, your original big CSV may still be better.

My recommendation

Use this order:

# 1. Test public S&P refresh
dailystonks-refresh-universe --source sp500 --dry-run

# 2. Actually write it
dailystonks-refresh-universe --source sp500

# 3. Launch app
stonks-live --tier black --tickers SPY,QQQ,AAPL,MSFT --interval 1d

Then, when you find your original big CSV, import it:

dailystonks-refresh-universe --source custom --input "D:\code\daily-stonks\path\to\your\original_universe.csv"

That gives you the best of both worlds: portable standalone app, but still able to use your proper curated DailyStonks universe.
